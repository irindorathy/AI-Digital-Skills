AI fluency is the ability to understand, evaluate, and responsibly use artificial intelligence in everyday life and professional settings. It goes beyond technical skills, empowering individuals to collaborate effectively with AI systems, make informed decisions, and uphold ethical standards. Key areas of AI fluency include foundational knowledge of how AI works, awareness of responsible AI principles like fairness and transparency, and practical skills for using tools like Microsoft Copilot or ChatGPT. Learners develop the ability to design effective prompts, interpret AI outputs, and assess risks such as bias or misuse. As AI becomes embedded in education, healthcare, finance, and governance, fluency ensures people can navigate these changes with confidence, creativity, and accountability.
