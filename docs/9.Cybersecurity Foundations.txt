Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, or damage. Its main goals are to ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information, often called the CIA triad. Cyber threats come in many forms, including malware, phishing, ransomware, hacking, and denial-of-service attacks, and they can target individuals or organizations. To defend against these threats, cybersecurity uses tools and strategies like firewalls, antivirus software, encryption, strong authentication, access controls, and regular updates. Following best practices—such as using strong passwords, enabling multi-factor authentication, backing up data, and staying alert to social engineering—helps protect sensitive information and keep digital systems safe.
