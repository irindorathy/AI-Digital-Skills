Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, or damage. Its main goals are to ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information, often called the CIA triad. Cyber threats come in many forms, including malware, phishing, ransomware, hacking, and denial-of-service attacks, and they can target individuals or organizations. To defend against these threats, cybersecurity uses tools and strategies like firewalls, antivirus software, encryption, strong authentication, access controls, and regular updates. Following best practices—such as using strong passwords, enabling multi-factor authentication, backing up data, and staying alert to social engineering—helps protect sensitive information and keep digital systems safe.
Importance of Cybersecurity:Cybersecurity is important because many organizations, governments, and individuals depend on digital systems to store and manage information. Without proper security, sensitive data such as financial records, passwords, and personal information can be stolen or misused. Strong cybersecurity measures help prevent data breaches, protect privacy, and maintain trust in digital systems.
Cybersecurity foundations include important practices such as risk management, access control, network security, and security policies. Risk management helps identify and reduce potential cyber threats, while access control ensures that only authorized users can access systems and data. Network security protects computer networks from attacks and unauthorized access. Security policies provide guidelines for safe use of digital systems. In addition, incident response plans help organizations respond quickly to cyberattacks, and user awareness programs educate people about online threats like phishing and malware.
